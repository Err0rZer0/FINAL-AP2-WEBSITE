About Rizal Tourism

Discover the gateway to the Sierra Madre and the artistic soul of the Philippines.

Province Overview

A vibrant escape for art, nature, and heritage travelers

Rizal Province blends easy access from Metro Manila with inspiring art experiences, lush mountain trails, refreshing waterfalls, and authentic local cuisine.

Location

Rizal sits directly east of Metro Manila in CALABARZON, bordered by Laguna de Bay to the west and the Sierra Madre mountains to the east.

Background & History

Named after national hero José Rizal, the province is known for its artistic heritage, historic pilgrimage sites, and a thriving tradition of faith and creativity.

Known Attractions

  • • Masungi Georeserve and Pamitinan Peak
  • • Daranak and Hinulugang Taktak waterfalls
  • • Angono art galleries and Higantes Festival
  • • Talim Island lakeside escapes and local food trails

Culture & Traditions

Rizaleños celebrate artistry, faith, and craftsmanship through festivals, folk rituals, and vibrant street art that reflect the province’s warm community spirit.

Why Tourists Should Visit

Choose Rizal for a polished provincial getaway with nature, culture, history, and gastronomy all within a short ride from the capital.

Fast Access

Less than 2 hours from Manila for a convenient day trip or weekend escape.

Authentic Food

Taste homemade rice cakes, savory specialties, and market finds prepared by local communities.

Cultural Events

Enjoy vibrant festivals, art shows, and traditional celebrations with warm local hospitality.

Nature Trails

Explore waterfalls, rivers, mountains, and island shorelines in a single province.
